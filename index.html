Nisab (Minimum Threshold) Definition

Based on authentic scholarly consensus, Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth that triggers Zakat obligation:

  • Gold-based Nisab: 87.48 grams (2.5 Mithqal)
  • Silver-based Nisab: 612.36 grams (52.5 Dirham)
  • Application: Use whichever is lower in current market value
  • Source: Fiqh consensus from major schools (Maliki, Hanafi, Shafi'i, Hanbali)

Zakat Rate & Eligible Wealth

  • Standard Rate: 2.5% per lunar year (Rub' Al-'Ushr)
  • Applicable Assets:
    • Gold and silver (jewelry, bullion, coins)
    • Cash and monetary assets
    • Business inventory and merchandise
    • Stocks, bonds, and investments
    • Rental income receivable
  • Excluded Assets:
    • Personal residence (primary home)
    • Personal vehicles and transportation
    • Household furnishings and personal items
    • Professional tools and equipment
    • Land for personal use (not investment)

Calculation Period

  • Zakat Year: 1 Lunar (Islamic) Year = 354-355 days
  • Hawl (Possession Period): Wealth must be held for a complete lunar year
  • Anniversary Date: Zakat is typically due on the same date (Hijri calendar) each year
  • Source: Hadith in Sunan Ibn Majah 1790 and Sunan At-Tirmidhi 620

Zakat Recipients (Asnaf Al-Zakat)

As mentioned in Surah At-Taubah 9:60, Zakat is for:

  • Al-Fuqara (the extremely poor)
  • Al-Masakin (the poor)
  • Aamileen (Zakat administrators)
  • Mu'allafat Al-Qulub (those whose hearts are inclined to Islam)
  • Ar-Riqab (those in bondage)
  • Al-Gharimin (those in debt)
  • Fi Sabilillah (in the path of Allah)
  • Ibnu As-Sabil (travelers in need)

Scholarly Schools (Madhhabs) on Zakat

  • Maliki School: Strict on trade goods valuation, includes all merchandise
  • Hanafi School: Requires completion of full lunar year, includes most investments
  • Shafi'i School: Similar to Hanafi with minor differences on some assets
  • Hanbali School: Includes livestock and agricultural produce calculations
  • Note: This calculator follows the consensus approach used by most contemporary Islamic scholars
